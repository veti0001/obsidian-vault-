
- Stress testing:
	- use to evaluate the potential effects of a change of assumption on a company financial situation
	- exceptional but plausible events
	- usage:
		- new products
		- useful after long period of benign market condition
	- Useful for all type of model
	- helps sets institution's risk appetite, setting exposure limits, and evaluating strategic choices in longer term business planning.
	- Purpose:
		- **Risk identification and control**
		- **Providing a complementary risk perspective to other risk management tools**
		- **Improving liquidity management**
	- Role of senior management
		- essential for the results
	- General Consideration
		- different model and expert involve 
		- governance around program
		- reusable pipeline and infrastructure
		- maintain pipeline
	  Risk to consider:
		- all risk that are material for the company
		- also include  non historical scenario , but needs to be possible
		- take into account corelation between risks
		- risk mitigation
		- capture risk to reputation
	- OSFI review stress testing of company
		- look at multiple things
		- describe at the end of this document ([Stress Testing – Guideline (2009) - Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions](https://www.osfi-bsif.gc.ca/en/guidance/guidance-library/stress-testing-guideline-2009))

- Reverse stress test:
	- starts with specified outcome that challenge viability of firm
	- then works backwards to identify what can cause that issue
