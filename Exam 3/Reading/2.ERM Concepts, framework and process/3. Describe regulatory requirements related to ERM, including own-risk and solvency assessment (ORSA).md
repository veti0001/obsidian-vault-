- ORSA represent the insurer own view of his risk and solvency assessment
- Help insurers understanding of the relationship between risk profile and capital needs
- Stress and scenario testing is important
- Scope:
	- consider all possible risk an insurer (subsidiary included) can face
	- can also be more specific to help the company set internal target
	- ORSA = set target and assess material risk
- key element :
	- Comprehensive Identification and Assessment of Risks
		- include all material risk
		- also take into account combination of risk, that are material
	- Relating to risk capital:
		- set internal target
			- based on assessment of own capital needs
				- stress and scenario testing
				- More material risk = more complex method
				- take into account relevant literature
			- compare to expected needed capital
			- consider adverse scenario on capital needed
	- Should coincide with the company business plan and ERM
	- Identify relevant measures to improve solvency
	- Oversight:
		- Improve accuracy and effectiveness of ORSA
	- Monitoring and reporting
		- perform on regular basis
		- clearly documented
	- Internal Controls and objective review:
		- reviews regularly to have an idea of the soundness of pipelines
- Requirements:
	- Sufficient capital to meet Minimum and Supervisory target regulatory capital
	- OSFI can review and insurer ORSA report
- Third Party arrangement:
	- provide information regarding those arrangement to OSFI upon requests
	- include: critical services for the FRFI, minor support arrangements, and strategic arrangements
	- some arrangement can be riskier than others and we need to take that into account
	- outcomes:
		1. Governance and accountability structures are clear with comprehensive risk management strategies and frameworks in place.
			- Company is responsible for managing third party risks
			- Have policies in place to mitigate those risks
		2. Risks posed by third parties are identified and assessed.
			- concentration risk is also assessed
			- also take subcontracting risk into account
			- should have contingency/exit plan
		3. Risks posed by third parties are managed and mitigated within the FRFI’s risk appetite framework.
		4. Third party performance is monitored and assessed, and risks and incidents are proactively addressed.
		5. The FRFI’s third-party risk management program allows the FRFI to identify and manage a range of third-party relationships on an ongoing basis.
		6. Technology and cyber operations carried out by third parties are transparent, reliable and secure.
- Environmental risks:
	- Company needs to report to OSFI on the risk on a periodical basis (climate scenario)
- What to disclose:
	- relevant info
	- specific and comprehensive
	- understandable info
	- reliable and verifiable info
	- appropriate info for company size
	- disclose consistent info over time
