- Cyber and technology risks:
	- no perfect solution
	- Technology risks: 
		- which includes “cyber risk”, refers to the risk arising from the inadequacy, disruption, destruction, failure, damage from unauthorised access, modifications, or malicious use of information technology assets, people or processes that enable and support business needs, and can result in financial loss and/or reputational damage
	- taken into account like any other risk
	- Senior management are managing this risk
	- Have a reliable plan
	- a company should have a technology and cyber risk management framework (risk appetite for technology risks)
	- have are reliable technology environment 
	- have a view of all the company technology (to be certain that they are manage well)
	- have measure in place to mange technology incident
	- have a disaster recovery program 
	- be cyber secure
	- learn from past incident to be better in the future
- Climate risk
	- physical risk:
		- financial risk arising from higher severity and frequency
	- transition risk;
		- financial risk arising from transitioning to green energy (Legislation, policies, customer sentiment, changes in market)
	- risk should increase over time
	- no perfect approach
	- outcomes:
		1. The FRFI understands and mitigates against potential impacts of climate-related risks to its **business model and strategy**.
			1. Senior management responsibility
			2. take into  account those risks in the strategy of the company
			3. Manage risk in accordance with the risk appetite of the company
			4. Monitor those risks
			5. use scenario analysis
			6. have enough capital to cover those risks
		2. The FRFI has appropriate **governance and risk management practices** to manage identified climate-related risks.
		3. The FRFI remains **financially resilient** through severe, yet plausible, climate risk scenarios, and **operationally resilient** through disruption due to climate-related disasters.
